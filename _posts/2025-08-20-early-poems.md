**Owner of Ease**
<br>
Books and books 
and books and books -
I own all of these.
In stacks and stacks
they grant me facts
I studiously know can
grant me ease.

Earliest morn my death 
was born as fire
grasped my life.
In strife 
the books had lit the night -
my heart taut and tight.

Once burned I turned
and cried and jumped.
Oh what am I to do?

but then what's seen -
a smoky sheen
by pulled all the way through.

Zealous much and 
by rebellion such
I empty walked along.

Struggle-studious
with open ears
I came upon a song. 
I walked a circle 
tough and smooth
for all the day ahead.

The circle told -
voiceless bold -
that what I can see,
I can shed.
Shook down with eyes 
shook off with lies, 
but what was left for me?

Life limber, 
shake anew. 
Well - 
it's to stroll 
through coals
with glee.
